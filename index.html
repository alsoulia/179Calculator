What is Section 179?

Section 179 of the IRS tax code allows businesses to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment and software purchased or financed during the tax year. Instead of depreciating the asset over several years, you can write off the entire cost immediately, significantly reducing your taxable income.

2026 Limits and Rules

  • Deduction Limit: For 2026, the deduction limit has increased to $1,340,000.
  • Spending Cap: The phase-out threshold begins at $3,350,000 in total equipment purchases.
  • Bonus Depreciation: Available for assets that exceed the Section 179 limit.

Qualifying Equipment

Most tangible goods used by your business qualify, including:

  • Heavy machinery and manufacturing equipment.
  • Computers, servers, and "Off-the-Shelf" software.
  • Office furniture and business vehicles (subject to weight limits).
  • Large printing presses and medical equipment.
